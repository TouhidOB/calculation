Sharpe Ratio Calculator

Determine the **Sharpe Ratio**, which measures the return earned in excess of the risk-free rate for every unit of total risk (volatility) taken.

Sharpe Ratio Formula:

$$S_a = \frac{R_a - R_f}{\sigma_a}$$ Where: $R_a$ = Portfolio Return, $R_f$ = Risk-Free Rate, $\sigma_a$ = Standard Deviation (Volatility)

Interpreting Your Sharpe Ratio:
  • Ratio $\ge$ 2.0: **Very Good.** Excellent risk-adjusted performance.
  • Ratio $\ge$ 1.0: **Good.** The investment provides adequate excess return for the risk taken.
  • Ratio $<$ 1.0: **Suboptimal.** The excess returns may not justify the level of volatility.

**Higher is always better.** The ratio allows you to compare different investments on a risk-adjusted basis; the one with the higher Sharpe Ratio is the better performer.
