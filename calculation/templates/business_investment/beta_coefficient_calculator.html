Beta Coefficient ($\beta$) Calculator

Calculate the **Beta ($\beta$)** coefficient, which measures a stock's **systematic risk**â€”how sensitive its returns are to changes in the overall market.

Beta Formula:

$$\beta = \frac{\text{Covariance}(\text{Stock}, \text{Market})}{\text{Variance}(\text{Market})}$$

Statistical Inputs (from Return History)
Cov

Measures how the stock's returns move together with the market's returns.

Var

Measures the market's overall volatility. This value **must be positive**.

Interpreting Your Beta ($\beta$):
  • Beta > 1.0: The stock is **more volatile** than the market. It tends to rise and fall by a larger percentage than the market index. (Higher Systematic Risk)
  • Beta $\approx$ 1.0: The stock moves **in line** with the market. If the market rises by 1%, the stock is expected to rise by approximately 1%. (Average Systematic Risk)
  • Beta < 1.0 (but $>$ 0): The stock is **less volatile** than the market. It offers a defensive position during market downturns. (Lower Systematic Risk)
  • Beta $\leq$ 0: The stock moves **opposite** to the market (or has no correlation). This is rare and highly desirable for diversification.
