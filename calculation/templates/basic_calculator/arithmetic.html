What is Arithmetic?

Arithmetic is the oldest and most fundamental branch of mathematics. It involves the study of numbers and the traditional operations performed on them: addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division.

Basic Arithmetic Operations

Our calculator handles the four core operations that form the basis of all mathematics:

Addition (+)

Combining two or more numbers to find a total sum.

Example: 5 + 3 = 8

Subtraction (−)

Finding the difference between two numbers.

Example: 10 − 4 = 6

Multiplication (×)

Repeated addition of a number.

Example: 4 × 3 = 12

Division (÷)

Splitting a number into equal parts.

Example: 20 ÷ 5 = 4

Order of Operations (PEMDAS/BODMAS)

When performing calculations with multiple operations, the order matters. The standard rule is known as PEMDAS or BODMAS:

  • P/B: Parentheses / Brackets
  • E/O: Exponents / Orders (Powers and Roots)
  • MD/DM: Multiplication and Division (left to right)
  • AS: Addition and Subtraction (left to right)
Note: This basic calculator performs operations immediately as you type. For complex expressions requiring strict order of operations, use a Scientific Calculator.
